History of Yambol

Home | History | Places to Visit

Ancient Times

Yambol was originally founded as Dionysopoulos by Greek settlers around 397 BC. The city was later renamed Yambol by the Thracians and became an important settlement in the region.

Roman Period

During the Roman Empire, Yambol became part of the province of Thrace. It served as a significant trade center along the routes connecting the Mediterranean and the Black Sea regions.

Ottoman Rule

From the 14th century until 1878, Yambol was under Ottoman rule. During this period, the city developed strong commercial activity and was known for its markets and crafts, particularly textile production.

Modern Era

Yambol was liberated from Ottoman rule during the Russo-Turkish War of 1877-1878. After Bulgaria's independence, Yambol became an important industrial and cultural center.

Historical Significance

  • Home to ancient ruins and archaeological treasures
  • Preserved Ottoman architecture and monuments
  • Important center for Bulgarian national revival
  • Rich cultural traditions passed down through centuries
